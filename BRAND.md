# SnapBar Brand — "Contact Sheet"

The identity is borrowed from film photography's contact sheet: the printed grid
of frames a photographer marks up with a grease pencil, circling the keepers.
A screenshot app is exactly that — frames of your screen, marked up and picked.

It is deliberately the opposite of the "AI default" look (dark page, indigo→purple
gradient, glassy cards, Inter, emoji icons). Everything here is flat, printed,
warm, and slightly imperfect.

## Palette

| Name        | Hex       | Use |
|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Paper       | `#F2EEE3` | Backgrounds. Warm bone, never pure white. |
| Card        | `#EAE4D4` | Raised surfaces on paper. |
| Ink         | `#1A1713` | Text, borders, buttons. Never pure black. |
| Graphite    | `#6B6558` | Secondary text, captions. |
| Vermillion  | `#E14E1D` | The grease pencil. One accent, used sparingly: keeper circles, PRO tags, the record dot. |

Rules: no gradients, no glassmorphism, no glow. Dark sections are Ink with Paper
text — a negative, not a "dark mode".

## Type

- **Display:** Fraunces (72pt optical, SemiBold; italic for the accent word).
- **Labels / spec text:** IBM Plex Mono, always uppercase, letter-spaced, small
  (`SB-01`, `FIG. 02`, `MACOS 13+`). This is the voice of the brand.
- **Body:** system sans, small and utilitarian. The character lives in the
  display and mono layers, not the body.

## Logo

Four viewfinder crop-corner brackets around an off-center vermillion dot — the
frame you're about to take, with the keeper mark already inside. Drawn as flat
strokes with rounded caps; ink on paper (invert on ink). The app icon is the same
mark on a paper squircle.

## Motifs

- Crop marks / registration marks (⌖) on card corners
- Frame numbers (`SB-01`, `SB-02`…) as captions
- Hand-drawn vermillion ellipse circling the "keeper" (featured price, hero shot)
- Hairline ink rules; tables instead of floating cards
- Paper grain (very low-opacity noise) over large surfaces
- Square corners (2px), hard 2px offset shadows — stamped, not floating

## Voice

Specific over aspirational. "4 MB, no Electron, no cloud" beats "Build the
future". Photography vocabulary where it's natural (frames, rolls, keepers,
darkroom) — never forced.
