Questions

Q.01 How is this different from ⇧⌘5? +

SnapBar uses the same rock-solid macOS capture engine underneath, then adds the whole darkroom: instant markup with step numbers, OCR text grabbing, pinned reference shots, share-ready backdrops, a draggable floating frame, capture history and global hotkeys — one click from your menu bar.

Q.02 Do my screenshots leave my Mac? +

No. Everything, including OCR, runs on-device. Captures go to the folder you pick and nowhere else. There's no account, no telemetry, no cloud.

Q.03 What happens after the 7-day trial? +

Capture, recording, the markup editor and the floating frame stay free forever. The Pro tools — OCR, Pin, Beautify, Pixelate — lock until you buy a license, one-time or monthly.

Q.04 Is the one-time purchase really lifetime? +

Yes. $14.99 once, every future update included. The monthly plan exists purely for people who prefer small payments — it's the same feature set.

Q.05 Which Macs does it run on? +

macOS 13 Ventura or newer, Apple Silicon and Intel. It's a small native Swift app — about 4 MB on disk, no Electron, no background CPU burn.

Q.06 What permissions does it need? +

Screen Recording — that's the whole point — and Microphone only if you turn on mic audio for recordings. macOS asks the first time, and that's it.

Q.07 macOS says it "can't verify the developer"? +

SnapBar isn't notarized through Apple yet (that program costs $99/yr — it's on the list). First launch only: right-click SnapBar.app → Open → Open. The app is open source, so you can also read every line and build it yourself.
